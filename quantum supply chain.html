🟡 Strategic chokepoint Helium-3 comes almost exclusively from tritium decay in U.S. and Russian nuclear programs — about 8kg produced globally per year. Each dilution refrigerator uses ~100L of He-3 per fill cycle. As quantum data centers scale, He-3 becomes a genuine bottleneck with no easy substitute.

🔴 The dilution refrigerator oligopoly — the most direct "picks and shovels" play Bluefors + Oxford Instruments control ~55% of the global dilution refrigerator market. 🟡 The architecture bet — nobody knows who wins Google proved below-threshold error correction. IonQ has the best fidelity. QuEra demonstrated 96 logical qubits. Microsoft's topological approach could leapfrog all of them — or fail entirely (experts are split). IBM has the clearest commercial roadmap. Betting on one architecture is like betting on one OS in 1980. The safer bet is the enabling layer.

The laser supply squeeze nobody is talking about A single trapped-ion quantum computer requires 5–10 precisely tuned laser systems across UV to near-IR wavelengths. TOPTICA (private) dominates this market. As IonQ, Quantinuum, and others scale from dozens to thousands of ion traps, laser supply becomes a genuine manufacturing bottleneck — and TOPTICA will likely IPO before 2028.

🔮 The 10-year bull case McKinsey projects $2.7 trillion in economic value from quantum computing by 2035. Drug discovery is first — simulating molecular structures at the quantum level could compress drug development from 12 years to 3. Financial modeling, logistics, and materials science follow. The AI analogy: 2015 NVDA is the control/foundry layer in 2026. The question is whether quantum reaches 2020-level adoption by 2030 or 2040.
